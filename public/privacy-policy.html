Privacy Policy

Last Updated: November 13, 2025

1. Introduction

Welcome to HealthCanvas. We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring the security of your personal and health information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our electronic health record (EHR) and practice management system.

HIPAA Compliance: HealthCanvas is designed to be compliant with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA). We implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to protect your Protected Health Information (PHI).

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Personal Information

  • Name, date of birth, gender, and contact information
  • Social Security number and government-issued ID numbers
  • Insurance information and billing details
  • Emergency contact information

2.2 Health Information

  • Medical history, diagnoses, and treatment plans
  • Medications, allergies, and immunization records
  • Laboratory and diagnostic test results
  • Clinical notes and documentation
  • Appointment history and scheduling information

2.3 Technical Information

  • IP address, browser type, and device information
  • Login credentials and authentication data
  • Usage data and system logs

3. How We Use Your Information

We use your information for the following purposes:

3.1 Treatment

  • Providing, coordinating, and managing healthcare services
  • Consultation between healthcare providers
  • Referrals to other healthcare professionals

3.2 Payment

  • Billing and collecting payment for services
  • Processing insurance claims
  • Determining eligibility and coverage

3.3 Healthcare Operations

  • Quality assessment and improvement activities
  • Training and education of healthcare professionals
  • Business planning and administrative functions
  • Customer service and support

4. Information Sharing and Disclosure

We may share your information in the following circumstances:

4.1 With Your Authorization

We will obtain your written authorization before using or disclosing your PHI for purposes other than treatment, payment, or healthcare operations, except as described in this policy or required by law.

4.2 Without Your Authorization

We may disclose your information without authorization in these situations:

  • Required by Law: When disclosure is required by federal, state, or local law
  • Public Health: To prevent or control disease, injury, or disability
  • Health Oversight: To authorized health oversight agencies for audits and investigations
  • Legal Proceedings: In response to court orders or subpoenas
  • Law Enforcement: For certain law enforcement purposes
  • Serious Threats: To prevent a serious threat to health or safety

4.3 Business Associates

We may share your information with third-party service providers (Business Associates) who assist us in operating our system. These include:

  • Cloud hosting providers
  • Payment processors
  • Email and communication services
  • Data backup and security services

All Business Associates are required to sign agreements ensuring HIPAA-compliant handling of your information.

5. Your Rights

Under HIPAA, you have the following rights regarding your health information:

5.1 Right to Access

You have the right to inspect and obtain a copy of your health records.

5.2 Right to Amend

You may request amendments to your health information if you believe it is incorrect or incomplete.

5.3 Right to an Accounting

You have the right to receive an accounting of certain disclosures of your PHI.

5.4 Right to Request Restrictions

You may request restrictions on how we use or disclose your PHI for treatment, payment, or healthcare operations.

5.5 Right to Confidential Communications

You may request that we communicate with you in a specific way or at a specific location.

5.6 Right to Notification of Breach

You have the right to be notified in the event of a breach of your unsecured PHI.

6. Data Security

We implement comprehensive security measures to protect your information:

  • Encryption: Data is encrypted both in transit (using TLS/SSL) and at rest
  • Access Controls: Role-based access controls and authentication mechanisms
  • Audit Logs: Comprehensive logging of system access and activities
  • Regular Security Assessments: Periodic security audits and vulnerability assessments
  • Staff Training: Regular training on HIPAA compliance and security practices
  • Backup and Recovery: Regular automated backups with secure storage

7. Data Retention

We retain your health information in accordance with applicable laws and regulations:

  • Medical records: Minimum of 7 years after last treatment (longer if required by state law)
  • Billing records: As required by federal and state regulations
  • Audit logs: Minimum of 6 years as required by HIPAA

8. Children's Privacy

Our services are designed for use by healthcare providers treating patients of all ages. When treating minors, we comply with applicable state laws regarding parental access to minor children's health information.

9. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We reserve the right to modify this Privacy Policy at any time. We will notify you of any material changes by:

  • Posting the updated policy on our website
  • Updating the "Last Updated" date
  • Providing notice through the application

Changes will be effective immediately upon posting unless otherwise specified.

10. State-Specific Rights

Depending on your state of residence, you may have additional privacy rights under state law. These may include enhanced rights to access, delete, or control the use of your information. Please contact us to learn more about rights specific to your state.

11. International Users

HealthCanvas is designed for use in the United States. If you access our services from outside the U.S., please be aware that your information will be transferred to, stored, and processed in the United States where our servers are located.
